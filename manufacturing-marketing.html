Tactical Playbook
5 Plays Manufacturers Are Running Right Now
These aren't concepts. These are specific motions live at manufacturing companies today — mapped to the platform that runs them.
Play 01
Lapsed Buyer Re-Engagement — Fully Automated
A customer who hasn't ordered in 90 days triggers an automated journey: personalized email referencing their last order category, a follow-up SMS from their rep, and a Salesforce task for inside sales if still no response at day 14. Zero manual effort. Runs 24/7. Most manufacturing teams lose this revenue silently because no one has time to pull the list manually each week.
🏭 Platform: Marketing Cloud + Data Cloud
Play 02
Contract Renewal Runway — 90/60/30 Day Sequence
Data Cloud ingests contract dates from your ERP. When a contract hits 90 days out, a renewal journey launches automatically — value summary email at 90 days, case study or reference story at 60, direct outreach from the AE at 30. Marketing and sales moving as one coordinated motion, not a last-minute scramble.
🏭 Platform: Marketing Cloud + Data Cloud + CRM
Play 03
New Product Launch to a Segmented, Relevant Audience
Instead of blasting a new product announcement to your full list, Data Cloud segments by purchase history, industry, and product affinity. Customers who buy related products get the launch email. Everyone else doesn't. Engagement rates go up. Unsubscribes go down. The product actually launches to the buyers most likely to care.
🏭 Platform: Marketing Cloud Advanced + Data Cloud
Play 04
Agentforce Handles Inbound Marketing Inquiries Autonomously
A prospect fills out a "request a quote" form on your site at 9pm. Agentforce responds immediately — answers product questions, qualifies the opportunity, and either books a meeting or routes to inside sales with a full summary. No lead sits cold overnight. GE Appliances is resolving 25% of all interactions this way with no human involved.
🏭 Platform: Agentforce + Marketing Cloud
Play 05
Account-Based Marketing for Your Top 50 Accounts
Your top 50 accounts get a fundamentally different experience than the rest of your list. Data Cloud scores engagement and opportunity signals. Marketing Cloud triggers personalized content based on what that account is actually doing — visiting pricing pages, opening service emails, ordering specific SKUs. Marketing becomes a revenue signal, not just brand noise.

The Manufacturing Context
Why Manufacturing Marketing Is a Different Problem
Generic marketing platforms were built for B2C and SaaS. Manufacturing has a completely different set of constraints — and the platform has to match them.
Your best customers buy on relationship and reliability — not because they opened a marketing email. The platform has to support that reality, not fight it.

Manufacturing marketing isn't about volume. It's about being relevant at the right moment — before a contract comes up, after a product line expands, when a buyer at your key account changes roles. That requires data you actually have (ERP, CRM, service history) connected to the marketing platform in real time.

🔗
Your most valuable data lives in your ERP — not your marketing tool

Order history, contract dates, product affinity, customer tier — it's all in SAP or Dynamics. Data Cloud pulls it in and makes it actionable for marketing without a custom integration project.

👤
You're marketing to accounts, not individuals

A manufacturer with 300 accounts needs account-level intelligence — who's buying what, which accounts are growing, which are at risk. Marketing Cloud's account-based features are built for this motion, not a B2C email blast model.

🤝
Marketing and sales have to move together

In manufacturing, a marketing-qualified lead that doesn't get picked up by the rep within 24 hours is a dead lead. Marketing Cloud + Salesforce CRM shares the same data model — marketing triggers sales tasks, and sales activity informs marketing journeys.

⏱️
Long sales cycles mean nurture that actually runs

B2B manufacturing deals take 6–18 months. Most teams can't manually manage a nurture sequence over that timeline. Automated journeys that respond to actual account behavior — not just time-based drips — keep your brand in front of buyers through the whole cycle.
