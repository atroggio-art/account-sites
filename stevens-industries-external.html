Our Point of View

The ERP Runs the Business.
This Runs the Customer Relationship.

Stevens already has the right instinct. The question of whether to add a customer engagement layer isn't new. The hesitation has been about duplication: why add something that overlaps with what the ERP already tracks? The answer is that an ERP and a customer engagement platform aren't competitors. They solve completely different problems.

"Your ERP knows every order Stevens has ever shipped. But it has no idea that a prospect in your pipeline went quiet three weeks ago, that a rep just left and took their contact history with them, or that a key account hasn't been touched in 60 days."

That's not an ERP problem. It's a customer relationship problem, and it requires a different layer. The platform we're proposing sits on top of your ERP, not instead of it. It reads your order history and uses it to drive intelligent, automated outreach: re-engaging prospects before they go cold, keeping customers warm between rep visits, and giving management a unified view across all three sales groups without anyone duplicating data entry.

🚪
Every rep transition is a relationship at risk

When a rep changes territory or leaves, the contacts and conversations they managed go with them, unless they live in a system. A unified customer record means the next rep picks up exactly where the last one left off, with full context and no relationship gap.

🤖
Prospects go cold between rep touchpoints. Agents don't let that happen.

Custom manufacturing sales cycles are long. A prospect who's engaged today but doesn't hear back for three weeks is at risk. AI agents keep the conversation alive autonomously, sending the right follow-up at the right cadence, so reps re-engage warm prospects instead of cold ones.

👁️
Three sales groups, zero unified visibility

Management currently has no single view across casework, millwork, and other product groups: who's active, who's at risk, which accounts are overlapping. A unified customer layer gives leadership the cross-group visibility to make better decisions without asking reps to file more reports.

🔗
ERP data becomes a marketing asset, not just an operations record

Order history sitting in an ERP is operational data. The same data connected to an agentic outreach layer becomes a signal: a customer who bought casework 18 months ago and hasn't reordered is a re-engagement opportunity. That intelligence requires bridging the ERP, not replacing it.

Addressing the ERP Question Directly

Not a Replacement.
The Layer on Top.

The most common concern we hear from manufacturers with a strong ERP is: "We don't want to duplicate our data." That's the right instinct, and it's exactly what we're designed to prevent. The platform connects to your ERP rather than competing with it.

Your ERP owns transactions. This owns the customer relationship. They do different jobs, and together they're more powerful than either one alone.

📦
ERP: what was ordered, when, and for how much

Transactions, fulfillment, inventory, invoicing. The ERP does this better than anything else. We don't touch it.

🤝
Customer layer: who the relationship is with and where it's going

Prospect conversations, follow-up cadences, territory history, cross-group account visibility, and automated outreach. None of this lives in an ERP, and it shouldn't.

🔄
The integration is the value, not the duplication

When order history from the ERP flows into the customer layer, it becomes actionable: a lapsed account triggers an outreach, a rep sees full purchase context before a call, and AI identifies cross-sell based on what comparable customers have bought. No manual entry. No duplication.
