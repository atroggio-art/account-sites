The Real Problem
Why Quote-to-Cash Breaks Down in Manufacturing
Manufacturing has the most complex revenue motion of any industry. Engineered-to-order products. Tiered channel pricing. Multi-year contracts with escalators. Your quote-to-cash process carries that complexity — and usually carries it manually.
📋
Quoting complex products takes days when customers expect hours
An engineer-to-order quote that requires configuration input from three departments, pricing sign-off from a regional VP, and margin approval from finance does not move at the speed a competitive deal requires. By the time the quote goes out, your competitor has already followed up twice. Speed to quote is increasingly a competitive differentiator — not just a process improvement.
💸
Pricing inconsistency across channels is costing you margin every quarter
Direct sales, dealer network, distributor agreements, national accounts — each with different pricing tiers, discount authorities, and contract terms. When those price books live in spreadsheets and individual rep judgment, you get inconsistency. Reps who discount more close faster but leave margin on the table. Reps who hold price lose deals to teammates who bend the rules. Neither outcome is what you want.
🔄
Revenue recognition across long-term contracts is a manual reconciliation nightmare
A three-year contract with milestone billing, service components, and price escalators generates a revenue recognition schedule that someone has to maintain, update, and audit manually. When that contract amends, the schedule has to be rebuilt. Most finance teams are doing this in Excel, which means every audit is a fire drill and every amendment is a risk.
