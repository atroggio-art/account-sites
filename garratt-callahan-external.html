Our Point of View

Water Treatment Is a Trust Business.
But Trust Doesn't Scale Without a System Behind It.

Garratt-Callahan has built 90 years of credibility on the backs of technical experts who know water chemistry, know their accounts, and show up when it matters. The challenge isn't expertise. It's continuity. Between site visits, between service calls, between renewals, there's silence. That silence is where competitors get in.

"A facilities manager doesn't switch water treatment vendors because they found someone cheaper. They switch because the relationship went quiet, and a competitor was there when you weren't. Presence is the product."

Garratt-Callahan's commercial team manages accounts across industrial plants, commercial buildings, and institutional facilities. Each account has different decision-makers, different technical requirements, and different renewal timelines. Today, staying top-of-mind with all of them depends on individual rep memory and calendar discipline. That's not a system. It's a risk.

The platform we're proposing turns every account relationship into an automated sequence: the right content at the right time, renewal triggers 90 days before the window, cross-sell signals surfaced from service history, and every touchpoint logged so the next rep who touches the account knows the full history. Scale without losing the personal touch.

🔄
Renewal cycles run on rep memory. That's fragile.

Annual service contract renewals are the lifeblood of Garratt-Callahan's recurring revenue. When a rep leaves, gets promoted, or simply misses a window, that renewal is at risk. Automated nurture sequences tied to contract dates mean the account stays engaged regardless of rep turnover, and the renewal conversation starts before the window even opens.

🎯
Every account has a cross-sell opportunity the rep hasn't surfaced yet

A customer getting cooling tower treatment is a candidate for boiler chemistry. A facility on a chemical program is a candidate for testing services. Intelligent automation surfaces those signals from service history and usage data and delivers the right content to the right contact at exactly the moment they're most likely to engage.

📊
Marketing should tell sales who's ready, not the other way around

Today, reps decide who to call based on gut feel and spreadsheets. With behavioral engagement data (who opened what, who visited the site, who engaged with a regulatory compliance update), marketing hands sales a prioritized list of accounts showing buying signals. Reps spend time on the conversations most likely to convert.

🏭
Technical buyers want education, not promotion

Facilities managers and plant engineers don't respond to promotional emails. They respond to content that makes them better at their jobs: regulatory compliance updates, water chemistry guides, efficiency benchmarks. Personalized educational content built around Garratt-Callahan's technical expertise is the highest-value marketing asset this team has.

Account Nurture: Stay Present Between Every Visit
  • Automated email journeys triggered by account activity, service history, and contract milestones. No rep has to remember to follow up manually
  • Personalized content tracks by role: facilities managers receive compliance and efficiency content; plant engineers receive technical chemistry guides; procurement sees ROI benchmarks
  • Dynamic content blocks pull in account-specific data (water system type, service history, region) so every communication feels bespoke, not broadcast
  • Engagement scoring surfaces the accounts showing buying intent before the rep even picks up the phone
Renewal Automation: Never Miss a Window
  • Contract renewal triggers fire automatically at 90, 60, and 30 days with sequenced outreach that re-engages the account before a competitor gets there first
  • At-risk signals get identified from engagement drop-off, service ticket frequency, or price sensitivity indicators. The system flags accounts that need a human conversation, not just another email
  • Rep alerts route to the assigned technical rep when an account hits a renewal threshold or shows a disengagement pattern. Automation handles the cadence, humans handle the relationship
Cross-Sell Intelligence: Surface What the Rep Hasn't Asked For Yet
  • Service history analysis identifies accounts on one program (cooling tower) that match the profile of accounts successfully expanded to adjacent programs (boiler, steam, RO)
  • Automated cross-sell journeys deliver educational content about the adjacent solution before the rep is ready to have the conversation, creating the pull rather than just the push
  • Campaign performance dashboards show which content, which segments, and which journeys are generating the highest-quality pipeline so the team can optimize continuously, not just annually
