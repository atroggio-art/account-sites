Account Context
What you need to know before you pick up the phone
Who they are

North America's leading specialty distributor of composite materials. Fiberglass, carbon fiber, resins, tooling. 60+ distribution centers. Serving marine, wind energy, aerospace, automotive, and construction fabricators.

$554M revenue, 451 employees. HQ in Arlington Heights, IL. Family of brands includes Stratus (their marketing arm).

The real problem

Fabricators in their key verticals order by phone and email. Inside sales reps are an order desk. When a boat yard needs 50 gallons of resin at midnight, they call whoever answers first in the morning.

Account-specific pricing, volume agreements, and stocking deals are managed manually today. No self-serve ordering. No real-time inventory visibility for customers.

Why the prior pitches went dead

Two Commerce Cloud + MCA opportunities have gone "Dead - No Decision" since 2025. Not "No Opportunity" — No Decision. The play is real.

Most likely cause: internal champion couldn't build the business case or the deal got deprioritized. A Digital Transformation Program Lead (Rustagi) was added to Salesforce records in mid-2024. He may be the person who can move it this time.

What's different now

Jeff Benensohn came in as CIO in June 2025. New CIOs establish their platform roadmap in months 6-18. We're in that window.

Christina Chatfield has "eCommerce" in her marketing title — that's a signal the company is thinking about digital commerce. That role doesn't exist if there isn't a mandate somewhere.

Talk Track
Opener, discovery, and the pivot to value
The Opener (30 seconds)

"I work with specialty distributors across the West. The consistent problem I see is that fabricators who trust a distributor for 15 years still order through whoever is easiest to reach at midnight. I want to understand if that's a problem at Composites One, and whether it's something that's on your radar to fix."

Discovery Questions

What percentage of your inside sales team's time goes to reorder activity versus new account or cross-sell conversations?

How do your top fabricator accounts place reorders today?

Do customers have visibility into your DC inventory before they commit to a production run date?

What does your largest account's stocking agreement look like today, and who manages it?

The Value Pivot

"What you're describing is essentially your inside sales team acting as an order desk for accounts that already know what they want. Every one of those calls is time not spent on the conversations that actually grow the relationship."

"A portal that lets them log in, see their pricing, and place a PO with real-time inventory visibility handles all of that. The rep gets a notification. The order confirms. The rep's time goes to the accounts that need attention."

The Ask

"I'd like to show you what Westlake Royal built for their dealer and contractor channel. It's the closest structural analog to what Composites One could build for its fabricator base. 30 minutes. You tell me if it's directionally right or completely off."

Send the external briefing link as the pre-read: atroggio-art.github.io/account-sites/composites-one-external.html

Objection Handling
The responses that keep the conversation moving
We've looked at this before and it didn't go anywhere.
I know. Two conversations went to No Decision. That tells me the play is real but the business case didn't land internally. My ask is different: I want to show you what it looks like when it's working at a distributor your size, and let you tell me what's actually in the way. It might be budget, it might be integration complexity, it might be something I can help you solve. But "looked at it before" isn't a no from me.
Our fabricators prefer to call their rep. They like the relationship.
That's true for complex orders. It's not true at midnight for a reorder they've placed 20 times. The portal doesn't replace the rep. It handles the transactional work so the rep can do the relationship work. Sutton Tools found that when they gave customers a self-serve option, customers used the portal for routine orders and called the rep when they had a real question. Reps liked it more, not less.
We have our own eCommerce initiative in progress already.
Tell me more about where that's headed. Because the question I'd ask is whether account-specific pricing is connected to the ordering experience, or whether it's a catalog with generic pricing. That's the part most distributors build wrong the first time, and it's the part that determines whether your fabricators actually use it or keep calling.
Budget isn't there right now.
Understood. Can I ask where it sits on the priority list for next planning cycle? Because the math on this tends to be simple: if each of your top 100 fabricator accounts places one additional self-serve order per week, that number pays for the platform before you hit 12 months. I can put a business case together with your actual numbers if that would be useful for the internal conversation.
We don't want to disintermediate our sales team.
That's exactly the right concern to have. The distributors who get this wrong build a portal that competes with their reps. The ones who get it right build a portal that makes their reps look better: the customer gets an instant answer on inventory and pricing, the rep gets a notification when the PO is placed, and the rep's time goes to the orders that actually need a conversation. Pierre Janelle's team would be better off with this, not threatened by it.
