Our Point of View

The Showroom Is Your Superpower.
Commerce + Marketing Makes It Scale.

Arizona Tile wins on product quality, depth of selection, and the in-person experience. The gap most premium surface brands face is that the relationship ends when the customer walks out the door — unless you've built the digital infrastructure to keep it going.

"A contractor who walks your showroom, orders samples, and gets personally followed up by an AE is 3x more likely to specify your tile on the next project. Right now, that follow-up depends entirely on a human remembering to do it."

Marketing Cloud changes that equation. Every showroom visit, sample request, and spec sheet download becomes a trigger for a personalized, automated follow-up — so your best accounts stay warm without your team manually tracking every conversation. And when a designer at a firm specifies Arizona Tile on project one, B2B Commerce makes reordering so frictionless that project two, three, and four follow naturally.

🏪
55 showrooms generating data that disappears

Every showroom visit, sample pickup, and consultation is a signal. Without a connected platform, that intent data never makes it back to marketing or inside sales. Data Cloud captures it and makes it actionable across every channel.

🏗️
Trade accounts deserve a real B2B portal

Contractors, builders, and designers ordering at volume need account-specific pricing, project-based ordering, quote management, and order status — not a consumer checkout. B2B Commerce gives trade accounts the self-service tools they actually want.

✉️
New collection launches deserve more than a mass email

When Arizona Tile launches a new stone collection, the contractors most likely to specify it are already in your database — they've bought related SKUs, attended your showroom events, or downloaded similar spec sheets. Marketing Cloud segments and personalizes automatically.

🔁
Repeat business is the business — and it's automatable

A trade account that hasn't ordered in 60 days is a risk, not just a gap. Automated re-engagement journeys trigger on purchase history from your ERP — your team gets a list of exactly who to call, with full context, before the relationship goes cold.
