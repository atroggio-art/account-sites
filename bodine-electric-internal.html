Account Context
What you need to know before you pick up the phone
Who they are

Family-owned manufacturer of fractional horsepower motors and motor controls. Chicago, IL. Jeffrey Bodine is CEO. The Bodine family has run this company for generations and the name is literally on the product.

$122M revenue, 450 employees. Products span AC motors, DC motors, servo motors, brushless DC, gear motors, torque motors, and motor controls. OEM customers are in medical, machine tool, factory automation, office equipment, food processing, graphic arts, and packaging machinery.

The real opportunity

Bodine has one of the deepest fractional HP motor catalogs in North America. The problem is that OEM engineers finding the right motor spec is a phone call or a PDF search. When that process is harder than navigating a competitor's digital catalog, the spec goes to the competitor and every repeat order for the life of that production run goes with it.

A B2B Commerce motor configurator for OEM buyers is the lead. Let engineers filter by HP, torque, voltage, enclosure, and mounting style and get availability and lead times in real time. This is something Bodine's competitors do not have yet.

Why the prior plays went dead

Manufacturing Cloud has been pitched three times: 2023, 2024, and 2025. The first two were No Decision. The 2025 pitch got all the way to 50 seats and $83K and then went Dead Lost. Not No Decision. Lost. That means they evaluated and made a deliberate call to say no or go with something else.

The prior pitches were framed as internal tools for reps or back-office run rates. That is the wrong frame for a family-owned manufacturer. They care about what serves their OEM customers and protects revenue, not what makes their sales ops team more efficient.

What makes this play different

B2B Commerce as the lead has never been pitched to Bodine. A digital motor configurator is a customer-facing product decision, not an IT infrastructure project. It serves the OEM engineers who are your customer's buyers. That story resonates with Kevin Machalek (sales win rates), Terry Auchstetter (product experience), and even the CEO because it builds a competitive moat in a market where catalog depth only wins if buyers can find what they need.

Manufacturing Cloud as the complement, not the headline, handles run rate forecasting and volume agreements for the OEM accounts that commit to long production runs.

Talk Track
Opener, discovery, the value pivot, and the ask
The Opener

"Bodine has an exceptional motor catalog. The question I want to ask is how an OEM engineer at a packaging company finds the right Bodine motor today, and what happens when that process is harder than finding the same spec at a competitor."

That is the whole frame. Let them tell you what the process looks like in their own words before you say anything about what you sell.

Discovery Questions

How do OEM engineers typically start a spec at Bodine?

What is the ratio of spec calls that convert to an order?

When a medical OEM commits to a motor for a new device, what does the ongoing order relationship look like?

How do you track volume commitments from top OEM accounts?

The Value Pivot

"The spec decision is winner-take-all for a production run. If your catalog isn't as easy to navigate as your competitor's, you lose the spec and every repeat order for the life of that machine."

"A configurator doesn't replace your application engineers. It gets the right OEM buyers to the right engineer faster, with the preliminary spec already done. Your engineers' time goes to the applications where their expertise actually matters."

The Ask

"I'd like to give you a 30-minute demo of what a motor configurator looks like for an industrial manufacturer with complex product configurations. Not a general platform demo, a configurator built for the kind of spec decisions your OEM buyers are making."

If they engage, follow with: who else at Bodine should be in that conversation? Getting Machalek and Auchstetter in the same room is the goal.

Objection Handling
The five objections you will hear and how to move through them
We already looked at Manufacturing Cloud and it didn't work out.
The prior pitches were internal tools: Sales Cloud and Service Cloud for reps, and Manufacturing Cloud for back-office run rates. This is a customer-facing digital configurator for OEM buyers. It is a product that OEM engineers use, not one your reps use. Different value proposition, different stakeholders, different business case. The fact that Manufacturing Cloud went dead three times does not say anything about whether a digital catalog configurator creates value for Bodine's OEM customers.
Our customers prefer to work with our application engineers directly.
They prefer getting the right motor fast. Application engineers are the reason OEM buyers come to Bodine and they are a genuine competitive advantage. The configurator does not replace them. It handles the "do you make a motor that fits these parameters" question so the engineer's time goes to the applications where their expertise actually matters: complex configurations, edge cases, and the accounts where the relationship is the sale. The engineers' jobs get better, not smaller.
Our catalog is too complex for a digital tool.
The complexity is exactly why the digital tool creates value. A packaging OEM engineer who needs to filter by HP, torque, enclosure, and mounting configuration in 10 minutes has no patience for a PDF catalog. The manufacturers with the deepest catalogs benefit the most from a good configurator because the search problem is hardest for their buyers. Bodine's catalog complexity is not a barrier to building this. It is the reason this creates a competitive moat that a simpler catalog manufacturer cannot replicate.
We're not ready to make a platform decision right now.
Fair. Let me ask a different question: how are OEM engineers finding and speccing Bodine motors today compared to three years ago? If the answer is "the same way," that is the risk. The process your competitors are using has changed. I am not asking for a platform decision. I am asking for 30 minutes to show you what the competitive landscape looks like and let you tell me if it is relevant to Bodine or not.
We went with a different solution last time.
What was the deciding factor? That is genuinely useful to know. If it was price, I can work on that. If it was fit, I want to understand where the fit broke. If it was internal prioritization, I want to understand what changed. The fact that Bodine and Salesforce have had multiple conversations over the years tells me there is real interest here. I just want to understand what makes this one land differently. That conversation alone is worth 20 minutes.
