Our Point of View

The Most Precise Bioprocessing Products
in the World Run on a Generic CRM. That's the Problem.

Meissner's commercial team sells configured products into pharmaceutical and biotech manufacturing environments where precision, compliance, and traceability aren't optional. Microsoft Dynamics is a generic platform built for generic commercial processes. The mismatch isn't a minor inconvenience. It's a daily friction tax on the people doing the most specialized technical selling in the industry.

"When a biopharma process engineer configures a custom single-use assembly for a new drug manufacturing line, the quote needs to be right the first time. A revision cycle in a regulated environment isn't a delay. It's a compliance event."

Meissner's commercial complexity is structural. Every configured assembly is built from validated building blocks in a specific size range (5L–2,000L), sold into accounts with long-standing supply agreements and strict documentation requirements. The Athens expansion makes this more acute, not less. Scaling a commercial operation built on a generic CRM into a larger organization creates compounding risk at exactly the moment the business needs the most clarity.

What we're proposing replaces Dynamics with a purpose-built commercial platform: manufacturing-specific forecasting, run-rate tracking for consumable reorders, supply agreement management, and CPQ built for configured products. One platform, built for the complexity Meissner already lives every day.

🧬
Configured assembly quoting is a CPQ problem. Dynamics doesn't solve it.

Every Meissner order is a custom configuration: membrane type, housing, size, sterile connector specifications, all drawn from a validated component library. Quoting this in a generic CRM means manual configuration, manual validation, and revision cycles that slow down pharma customers running on tight manufacturing schedules. Purpose-built CPQ eliminates that entirely. The configuration is built once, validated automatically, and quoted accurately every time.

📈
The Athens expansion demands commercial infrastructure that can scale

A $250M campus investment in Athens, GA is a step-change in Meissner's production capacity and commercial footprint. Scaling a business of this magnitude requires commercial systems that grow with it: account-based forecasting that reflects both new wins and recurring consumable volume, and sales agreement management that can handle a broader account base without manual overhead. This is the forcing function for getting off Dynamics.

🔬
Consumable reorder revenue needs its own forecasting model

Filtration membranes and single-use consumables are a predictable recurring revenue stream. Bioprocessing facilities reorder on production schedules, not on impulse. Generic CRM pipeline forecasting treats these accounts the same as new business. Manufacturing Cloud's run-rate account model separates recurring consumable revenue from new project wins, giving the CCO and sales leadership a forecast that actually reflects how the business works.

📋
Supply agreements aren't just contracts. They need their own system.

Meissner's long-standing relationships with pharma and biotech accounts are governed by supply agreements with pricing, volume commitments, and compliance documentation. Managing these in Dynamics means living in spreadsheets attached to CRM records. Purpose-built sales agreement management gives the commercial team visibility into terms, compliance status, and renewal windows, without any manual tracking.
