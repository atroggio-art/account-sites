Our Point of View

Your CRM Knows What You Promised.
Your Plants Don't.

CSI Closures serves major food and beverage brands on volume-based contracts. Your customers don't order one-off — they commit to annual run rates, and you manufacture to those commitments. The gap is real-time visibility between what your commercial team has on the books and what your production teams are planning for. That gap is where margin leaks.

"A closure manufacturer's biggest risk isn't losing a customer. It's being surprised by a demand shift you had the data to see — if your CRM and your plants were talking to each other."

CSI already has strong CRM usage. Revenue Intelligence is running. The commercial team has the data. What's missing is the connection from commercial commitments to production planning — the piece that lets a plant manager see that a major F&B account's run rate just changed before it affects a production run.

Manufacturing Cloud bridges that gap. It doesn't replace your ERP. It connects your Salesforce data to the operational decisions your plants are already making, and gives your commercial team one view of account health that includes production performance, not just pipeline stage.

📊
Run rate agreements need real-time tracking, not quarterly reviews

Your F&B customers commit to volume. When a major account's actual orders start diverging from their run rate commitment, you need to know early — not at the end of the quarter when the variance is already locked in. Manufacturing Cloud tracks run rate agreements in real time and surfaces deviations before they become operational problems.

🏭
Commercial and operations need to see the same number

Right now your sales team is forecasting in Salesforce and your plants are planning from ERP data. When those two numbers diverge — and they do — someone finds out late. Manufacturing Cloud creates a shared view of account commitments and production plans so commercial and operations are always working from the same source of truth.

🤖
Your service team answers the same questions every day

Order status. Shipment timing. Spec confirmation for a new SKU. These are questions your customer service team fields dozens of times a day for F&B accounts who need answers before their production lines start. An Agentforce service agent handles those queries autonomously, 24/7, and escalates the ones that actually need a human — so your team focuses on the relationships, not the inbox.

🔄
You already have the data. You're just not using all of it.

Revenue Intelligence is already running on 30 seats. The underlying data is there. Manufacturing Cloud doesn't add a new data silo — it connects the commercial data you already have to the production and account performance layer. The lift is smaller than a greenfield implementation, and the value shows up fast because the CRM foundation is already solid.
