1
What's our POV?
CEO wants $20M → $100M with 3 reps. You can't hire your way there — you need systems that generate pipeline without adding headcount. Our angle: don't pitch this as a HubSpot replacement. HubSpot EOL and ZoomInfo expiring are the entry point, but the close is positioning MCA + Data Cloud + Agentforce as the GTM infrastructure that makes the $100M plan possible. One platform decision, not two vendor swaps.
2
Quick use cases: What are we selling? How will they use it?
  • Marketing Cloud Advanced: Automated account-based nurture journeys for distributors and direct accounts. Campaign triggers tied to sales activity in CRM (rep logs a call → journey pauses; prospect opens an email → task fires for rep). Targeted campaigns by vertical — HVAC, energy storage, industrial.
  • Data Cloud: Replaces ZoomInfo. Unifies CRM + website + campaign behavior into live prospect profiles with predictive scoring. Reps get account health scores and next-best-action — no manual list-building.
  • Agentforce: Inbound qualification agent handles "request a quote" and "contact us" forms 24/7 — responds, qualifies, books meetings with full context passed to the rep. A 3-person sales team that never misses an inbound lead.
3
What are the top 2–3 strategic priorities their execs are focused on?
  • Grow North American revenue $20M → $100M in 3–5 years — this is the CEO's mandate and the lens every technology decision should run through
  • Modernize the GTM motion — expand market coverage beyond 3 reps without a proportional headcount increase; automation and AI agents are the answer
  • Replace ZoomInfo with a reliable data and prospecting solution before the contract expires in ~4 months

Is there a compelling event or deadline driving urgency?
Two simultaneous forcing functions — rare window:

📅 March 2027 HubSpot marketing EOL. They must migrate off HubSpot — the question is what replaces it.

The framing that creates urgency: Don't let them solve these as two separate vendor decisions with two separate timelines. Position this as one platform decision happening right now — and the window to build the infrastructure for their $100M plan, not just swap tools.
6
What does success look like for your organization 12 months from now?
  • Marketing running automated nurture journeys for distributors, channel partners, and direct accounts — zero manual campaign execution
  • Inbound leads being qualified and routed by Agentforce — no lead goes cold overnight or over a weekend
  • ZoomInfo is gone. Prospecting powered by Data Cloud intelligence, fed directly into campaigns

  • The 3-rep North American team is operating with the leverage of a 10-person GTM org

1
What's our POV?
Stage 01 — we don't have a forcing function yet. The POV to validate on the first call: their buyer (truck owners) is digital-first, but Dee Zee's marketing data is split across dealers, e-commerce, and OEM. They can't do personalized lifecycle marketing without a unified consumer view. Lead with the post-purchase journey use case — it's concrete, easy to visualize, and immediately surfaces what their current stack can't do. Find the contract renewal on the first call.
2
Quick use cases: What are we selling? How will they use it?
  • Marketing Cloud Advanced: Automated post-purchase lifecycle journeys (bought a toolbox → 30-day nurture into bed rails and steps → product registration follow-up). Dealer co-op campaigns built and deployed centrally but personalized by dealer. Product launch sequences triggered across email, SMS, and paid retargeting simultaneously.
  • Data Cloud: Unified consumer profile across e-commerce, dealer POS, product registrations, and OEM channels. So marketing targets someone based on what truck they drive and what they already bought — not just a demographic. Real-time segmentation: truck owner who bought toolbox 6 months ago and hasn't registered or repurchased → re-engagement journey fires automatically.
3
What are the top 2–3 strategic priorities their execs are focused on?
  • Grow direct-to-consumer and digital marketing capabilities alongside their dealer channel — digital is now a primary discovery channel for their buyer
  • Build a unified customer view across dealer, OEM, and e-commerce purchase channels — they have data in silos and can't act on it
  • Operational improvement (Thomas Miller's mandate) — reduce manual marketing execution through automation; fewer tools, more output
4
Who are the key stakeholders and decision-makers we should be building relationships with?
  • Scott Moyer — Director of Marketing Primary champion. Owns the marketing platform decision. Lead with the omnichannel consumer journey use cases — post-purchase, dealer co-op, product launch automation.
  • Thomas Miller — Head of Operational Improvement Budget & efficiency angle. ROI story: fewer manual campaigns, more automated output, measurable attribution. He needs the business case numbers.
  • Shawn Schulz — Director OEM Sales & R&D Account-based and OEM channel angle. MCA can support dealer and OEM co-marketing programs — surfaces as a growth tool, not just a cost.
  • Jason Wiedmier / Akhilesh Neerukonda — Sr. IT Managers Technical evaluators. Active dev team signals implementation readiness. These are likely the fastest path to getting a POC scoped and moving.
5
Is there a compelling event or deadline driving urgency?
Stage 01 — no confirmed forcing function identified yet. Discovery questions to ask Scott Moyer on the first call:

  • "What's powering your marketing stack today — email, automation, CDP?" — find the Klaviyo / HubSpot / Segment contract
  • "When are you up for renewal on any of those contracts?" — contract renewal is the most likely urgency wedge
  • "Are you running any kind of post-purchase lifecycle marketing today, or is most of your effort going toward acquisition?" — surfaces the gap we fill
  • "How are you currently connecting what happens at a dealer to your own marketing data?" — opens the Data Cloud conversation
Most likely urgency path: Klaviyo or HubSpot renewal in the next 6–12 months. Find the contract date.
6
What does success look like for your organization 12 months from now?
  • Every truck buyer — whether they came through a dealer, Amazon, or deezee.com — has a single unified profile that marketing can act on
  • Post-purchase lifecycle journeys are running automatically: toolbox buyer gets a bed rails sequence 60 days later without anyone building a list
  • Dealer co-op campaigns are built once, deployed to 100+ dealers, personalized by territory — no manual dealer-by-dealer work
  • Attribution is real: Scott can show which campaigns drove which revenue, not just open rates
  • The marketing team is running strategy, not building lists — execution is automated
