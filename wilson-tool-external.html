Point of View
The ERP Transition Is the Forcing Function — Not the Blocker
Most manufacturers assume a platform change means freezing everything. The ones who come out ahead use the transition window to design a better customer experience from scratch.
Replacing your ERP is the right time to ask: what should our customers actually experience? — before you lock it in.

Wilson Tool's ERP transition opens a rare design window. The SAP commerce integration was described as a "great combination of a mess" — that's not a failure, that's evidence that commerce infrastructure needs to be rethought, not ported.

Salesforce B2B Commerce sits in front of your ERP — whatever ERP you run — handling the customer experience layer: browse, configure, price, order, account self-service. Your ERP handles fulfillment. They don't compete. They complement.

The question your Voice of Customer visits answered: your highest-volume customers depend on that portal. Shutting down commerce for a year doesn't just inconvenience them — it hands reorder volume to whoever makes it easiest to buy.

🏭
Configurable Products Need a Commerce Experience Built for Them

Wilson Tool's punch tooling, press brake tooling, and stamping dies span standard and custom-engineered SKUs. B2B Commerce with native CPQ handles complex configuration logic — so customers can self-configure and order without tying up inside sales on every quote.

👥
Your Sales Reps Should Be in Front of Customers, Not Entering Orders

61 direct reps across ~300 accounts each. When self-service handles reorders, quotes, and account documents, your reps stop being order clerks and start being the consultative partner your key accounts want to hear from.

📁
The Document Portal Is Commerce Infrastructure — Not a Separate System

Quotes, orders, invoices, drawings, CAD files — your customers built workflows around this. Salesforce Commerce brings self-service ordering and the account document portal into a single connected experience, not two separate products held together with integrations.

🔗
Commerce + Dynamics ERP: Not Either/Or

Salesforce Commerce connects to Dynamics ERP via standard APIs and MuleSoft connectors. Order data flows downstream to Dynamics for fulfillment. You get a world-class front-end buying experience without re-platforming your ERP investment.

What We're Recommending
The Platform Stack for Wilson Tool
Commerce as the starting point. Connected to your ERP, your service team, and your sales reps from day one.
Phase 1 — Foundation
B2B Commerce Cloud
Purpose-built for manufacturers selling direct and through distributors. Handles complex configurable products, account-based pricing, multi-level buyer hierarchies, and self-service order management.
  • Browse, configure, and order — any product complexity
  • Account self-service: orders, invoices, quotes, documents
  • Native CPQ integration for custom-engineered SKUs
  • Connects to Dynamics ERP via standard APIs
  • Customer portal + transactional commerce in one platform
Phase 1 — Accelerator
Agentforce Commerce
AI agents that handle routine customer interactions — order status, reorder, product questions — so your inside sales team handles exceptions, not volume. Deployed alongside B2B Commerce.
  • Agentforce handles reorder and order status 24/7
  • Autonomous support reduces inside sales ticket volume
  • Escalates complex or high-value cases to your team
  • Learns from every interaction to get smarter over time
Phase 1 — Integration
MuleSoft Anypoint
Connects Salesforce Commerce to Dynamics ERP for seamless order-to-fulfillment data flow. Pre-built Dynamics connectors accelerate time to go-live. No custom middleware to build or maintain.
  • Bi-directional Dynamics ERP sync — orders, inventory, pricing
  • Pre-built connectors reduce integration build time
  • API management for future systems (WMS, PLM, portals)
  • Replaces fragile point-to-point SAP integrations
