Account Snapshot

What They Do
Wilson Tool International manufactures precision punch tooling, press brake tooling, and stamping dies for sheet metal fabrication. They sell direct through 61 field sales reps (~300 accounts each) and through distributors globally. Products are highly configurable — standard and custom-engineered. Based in Saint Paul, MN with international manufacturing plants (Canada, Brazil, England, Italy, Germany).
Commerce Situation
Current commerce: SAP ECC + SAP HANA ("great combination of a mess" per Jeremy Edson). Expensive, difficult to integrate, no analytics. Being shut down. Replacement plan: Kentico for website/portal (already signed, under development), Dynamics CRM/ERP for backend. The portal (document repo: quotes, orders, invoices, drawings, CAD files) is mission-critical — customers use it heavily. Transactional commerce was tabled until 2027.
The Opening
Jeremy Edson explicitly flagged that Dynamics commerce UX is "not as robust as they desire from a consumer or customer standpoint." April VOC with high-volume customers was the circuit breaker — if customers push back on the commerce shutdown, Wilson has to move. The question is whether they build commerce on Dynamics or bring in a purpose-built B2B Commerce platform. Our answer: Salesforce B2B Commerce as the experience layer, Dynamics as the ERP backend. They don't have to choose.
CRM / ERP Context
Dynamics CRM is the incumbent. New Dynamics ERP finance layer is being added (kickoff March 2026). Jeremy Edson said the ERP selection makes it "harder to bring in Salesforce for CRM" — but remains open if Dynamics UX disappoints users. Don't try to displace Dynamics on this call cycle. Win Commerce, let the CRM story develop naturally.
