The Opportunity

You Built the Foundation.

Now Give Your Dealers a Better Way In.

Demco already has the hard part in place.

The CRM is live. The ERP is connected. The data exists.

What appears to be missing is the part dealers actually touch.

If they are still relying on calls, emails, spreadsheets, and someone internally to piece together every quote, that is probably creating a lot more work than it needs to.

The good news is this does not require starting over. It just means making the systems you already have easier for dealers to use.

What Stood Out to Us
01

The Data Is Already There

Because the CRM is connected to the ERP, much of the information dealers need should already be available.

Products. Pricing. Inventory. Order history.

Right now, that information may still require someone at Demco to track it down and send it over. A portal puts it directly in the dealer's hands.

Less chasing. Less waiting. Fewer emails that begin with "Just following up."

02

Another Standalone Tool Is Probably Not the Answer

A separate dealer platform may sound simple at first.

Then come the integrations, duplicate data, maintenance, and the inevitable debate over which system is actually right.

Suddenly, the tool built to simplify things becomes one more thing everyone has to manage.

Demco already has the platform. The smarter move is to build on it, not around it.

03

The Products Are Complicated Enough

Grain carts, sprayers, trailers, and all the possible configurations are not exactly an add to cart situation.

Dealers need to know what works together, what is available, and what it costs. Today, that may mean calling a rep and waiting while someone checks three different places.

That is fine once. It gets a lot less fine when it happens all day.

Guided selling could help dealers build the right configuration from the start while keeping your team out of cleanup mode. Because fixing a bad order after the fact is rarely anyone's favorite use of time.

The Point of View

This Is Not a Massive Transformation.
It Is the Next Logical Step.

Demco has already invested in the platform and the integration.

The opportunity now is to turn that investment into a better dealer experience.

Dealers get faster answers and an easier way to order. Sales gets fewer administrative requests. Operations gets cleaner orders. Leadership gets better visibility across the dealer network.

Everyone wins. Or, at the very least, everyone sends fewer emails.

What This Could Look Like

A Dealer Portal That Is Actually Useful

Each dealer logs in and sees the products, pricing, and information relevant to their account.

They can check availability, place orders, review past purchases, and track status without calling Demco every step of the way.

That means fewer interruptions for your team and fewer reasons for dealers to wonder what is happening. A surprisingly large part of customer experience is simply letting people know what is going on.

Guided Configuration Without the Guesswork

Instead of expecting every dealer to remember every product rule, the system walks them through it.

Compatible options appear. Invalid combinations do not. Pricing follows the right account logic. Orders arrive cleaner and require less review.

In other words, the system remembers the complicated stuff so people do not have to.

Better Visibility Into the Dealer Network

Once dealer activity flows through the platform, order data becomes much more useful.

You can see which dealers are growing, which products are moving, where demand is slowing, and where there may be an opportunity to step in.

The portal helps dealers buy. The data helps Demco sell. That is where this starts getting interesting.

The Next Step

Let's Map the Real Process

The best place to start is not with a generic demo. It is with the way dealers actually do business with Demco today.

Where do they get stuck? What creates the most calls? Which orders require the most hand holding? What information is hardest to find?

Once that is clear, we can map what a portal could look like using the systems and data Demco already has.

No giant transformation speech. No solution in search of a problem. Just a practical look at how to make Demco easier to do business with.
