Our Point of View

The Spec Decision Happens Online Now.
If Your Catalog Isn't Interactive, Your Competitor's Is.

OEM engineers in automation and packaging don't call vendors to start a spec anymore. They go online, filter by specs, compare options, and request quotes from whoever made it easy. Bodine has the product depth to win those specs. The question is whether the discovery experience matches the product quality.

"OEM engineers don't start the spec process with a phone call anymore. They go online, filter, compare, and shortlist. Bodine has the products to win those specs. The catalog experience is what decides whether they ever see those products."

A machine tool OEM in the Chicago suburbs is speccing a motor for a new axis. The engineer pulls up three options online, filters by HP, voltage, and enclosure type, and submits quote requests to two of them. The third supplier isn't in the running because their catalog is a 200-page PDF. This is happening right now, across all seven of Bodine's OEM verticals, every business day.

Bodine has been making fractional horsepower motors for OEM customers for decades. The product knowledge embedded in Bodine's application engineering team is genuinely hard to replicate. The problem isn't the products. It's that the spec discovery experience doesn't match the product quality. Competitors with digital configurators are winning spec decisions Bodine never even sees. The path forward is closing that gap before more production runs are locked in elsewhere.

⚙️
Application engineers are a competitive advantage. They are not an order desk.

When OEM buyers can self-serve the initial spec online, application engineers spend their time on the complex configurations where they add real value. Not answering "do you make a 1/3 HP 90VDC gear motor?" A digital configurator routes the right questions to the right people. The complex, high-value configurations go to your engineers. The standard specs get answered before the buyer ever picks up the phone.

🏆
The spec decision is winner-take-all for a production run.

When an OEM engineer specs a Bodine motor into a machine design, that motor gets ordered for every unit of that machine for years. Losing the spec decision doesn't cost one sale. It costs the entire production run. That's the real stakes of the PDF catalog problem. Every spec that goes to a competitor with a digital configurator is a production run Bodine will never recover. The spec window is open right now.

📊
Volume agreement tracking is manual today.

Repeat OEM customers who order the same SKU every quarter are being managed by reps in spreadsheets. When a top account's order pace drops below their committed run rate, your team finds out late. Run rate visibility means you see a production ramp before it hits your shop floor, and you see a pullback before the account has already made other arrangements.

📋
Medical and automation OEMs have strict documentation requirements.

A connected platform means compliance documentation, spec sheets, and order history live in one place for the OEM's procurement and engineering teams. Right now those are emailed PDFs that live in someone's inbox. When an OEM needs to requalify a motor supplier or pull historical order data for an audit, the experience they have with Bodine matters. Make it easy and you're the default supplier. Make it painful and you're at risk on the next design cycle.
