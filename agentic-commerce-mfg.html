The Real Problem
Why B2B Order Management Stays Broken
The tools most manufacturers use for order management were built for a world that does not exist anymore. Customers want to order the way they buy everything else. Your team wants to spend time on real work, not re-keying purchase orders.
📠
Fax and email orders are a full-time job your team cannot escape
For every order that comes in by phone, email, or fax, someone on your inside sales team manually keys it into your ERP. At 100 orders a week, that is one person's entire job, every week, forever. There is no strategic upside to that work. There is only risk: errors, delays, and a team that cannot focus on accounts that actually need attention.
🔒
Customers cannot see their own account without calling you
Order history, pricing, shipment status, open invoices. Your customers have to call or email someone on your team to get any of it. That is friction that costs you reorders. Distributors and dealers who can self-serve are more loyal, order more often, and do not churn to whoever answers the phone faster.
🌐
Your largest customers now expect a digital channel as table stakes
Enterprise buyers, national distributors, and dealer networks are increasingly mandating EDI or digital ordering as a procurement requirement. If you cannot meet that expectation, you lose the account to a competitor who can. This is not a future risk. It is already happening in RFPs and contract renewals across manufacturing right now.
