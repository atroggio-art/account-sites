Our Point of View

Agricultural Sales Has One Unforgiving Constraint:
The Seasonal Window.

Every ag company knows this: a deal that doesn't close before planting season doesn't close until next year. Manual quoting, email approval chains, and spreadsheet contract management aren't just inefficient. In ag, they're revenue risk with a hard deadline attached.

"In ag, speed-to-quote isn't a nice-to-have. If your field rep can't generate an accurate quote on-site and get it through approvals before the grower commits to a competitor, that deal is gone for 12 months."

Gowan sells through three distinct channel models: distributors, dealers, and direct grower relationships, each with its own pricing rules, contract terms, and renewal cadences. Managing all of that manually creates compounding risk. Pricing errors that erode margin, approval delays that miss windows, and renewal cycles managed in spreadsheets rather than triggered automatically.

The platform we're proposing automates quote-to-cash end-to-end. Accurate field quotes with real-time pricing, contract management built for multi-channel complexity, and billing that removes the manual reconciliation Gowan's finance team currently owns. One system, three channels, no seasonal deadline risk.

⏱️
Slow quotes cost deals, not just time

A field rep who can't generate an accurate quote instantly, with the right channel pricing, discounts, and terms, loses momentum. By the time it clears email approvals, the grower has moved on. Automated CPQ means the quote is right the first time and doesn't wait for anyone.

📋
Three channels, three contract models. One place to manage them.

Distributor agreements, dealer terms, and direct grower contracts have fundamentally different structures. Managing them across spreadsheets and email creates version-control nightmares and renewal risk. Contract lifecycle management gives Gowan's team a single source of truth across all three models.

💰
Manual billing is a tax on your finance team every season

Reconciling orders across three channels at the end of every season is a significant manual burden: matching invoices to contracts, processing exceptions, correcting pricing errors discovered after the fact. Automated billing eliminates that cycle and gives finance real-time visibility into what's owed.

🔄
Renewals that run themselves

Multi-year grower agreements and distributor contracts have renewal windows. Today those renewals depend on someone remembering to follow up. Automated renewal triggers mean no contract expires without an active conversation, and no revenue walks out the door because of a missed calendar alert.

CPQ: Accurate Field Quotes, Every Time
  • Reps generate accurate quotes on-site from mobile with real-time channel pricing. Distributor, dealer, and direct grower rates all pre-configured.
  • Approval workflows route automatically based on discount thresholds. No email chains, no waiting for a manager to be reachable.
  • Product bundles and crop-specific configurations built once and reused. No manual entry errors on complex multi-product orders.
  • Quote-to-contract in a single click once approved. No re-keying, no version control issues.
Contract Lifecycle: One System for Three Channel Models
  • Distributor agreements, dealer terms, and direct grower contracts all managed in one place. No spreadsheets, no shared drives.
  • Automated renewal triggers alert the account team 60–90 days before a contract expires. No renewal gets missed because someone forgot to set a calendar reminder.
  • Amendment management built in. Price adjustments and term changes are tracked and signed without breaking the original contract record.
Billing: Eliminate the End-of-Season Reconciliation
  • Automated invoicing triggered directly from contract milestones and delivery events. No manual invoice generation.
  • Pricing exceptions and adjustments get resolved at the point of sale, not discovered during month-end reconciliation.
  • Finance gets real-time visibility into outstanding receivables across all three channels. Seasonal forecasting becomes data-driven instead of gut-feel.
