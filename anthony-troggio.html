What I Help Solve

Four problems manufacturers bring to me most.

01

Slow, manual quote-to-cash

Complex configurations, multi-tier pricing, and approval chains that stretch a quote from hours to days. Competitors with automated CPQ close while you are still routing approvals.

02

Dealer and distributor ordering that depends on reps

Channel partners who have to call or email to place routine orders. Every one of those touches is friction you are paying for in rep time and order delays.

03

Marketing that does not connect to the commercial stack

Campaigns running in one system, CRM in another, ERP in a third. Leads get dropped. Follow-up is manual. The handoff between marketing and sales is a spreadsheet.

04

AI interest with no clear starting point

Leadership has decided AI matters. Nobody has a concrete answer for which process to automate first, what it costs, and what a realistic return looks like in year one.

Specialties

Four areas where I spend most of my time.

These are not broad platform capabilities. These are specific motions I help manufacturing teams build and run.

01

Agentic Marketing

Use AI to qualify inbound faster, segment audiences based on real behavior, and trigger follow-up without waiting on a rep to notice a signal. Marketing that responds in hours, not the next morning when someone checks a dashboard.

02

Quote to Cash

Remove manual steps from configuration, pricing approval, and contract execution in complex manufacturing sales. Reduce quoting cycles from days to hours. Enforce pricing policy without relying on rep judgment.

03

Agentic Commerce

Help dealer and distributor channels place routine orders without rep intervention. Guided product selection, dynamic pricing by channel tier, and self-service reorders. Revenue that moves without a human in the middle of every transaction.

04

AI & Agentforce

Identify one high-volume manual process, deploy an AI agent against it, and prove the return before expanding. Not a strategy workshop. A scoped production deployment with a clear outcome in the first quarter.

Who I Work With

When there is a fit, it usually looks like this.

Not every company is the right match. I would rather tell you that upfront.

01

Mid-market and enterprise manufacturers ready to move

Companies with 200 to 5,000 employees that have outgrown spreadsheets and disconnected point solutions. You have the operational complexity to benefit from automation and the urgency to actually do something about it this year.

02

Leaders who own the problem and can move on it

VPs and Directors of Sales, Marketing, Operations, and Finance who already know something is broken and have the authority to fix it. You do not need to be convinced the problem exists. You need a clear path and an honest view of what it takes.

03

Companies past "should we look at AI" and into "what does this actually look like"

You are not debating whether AI matters. You already know it does. You want to see a specific motion, in your industry, that has already proven itself, and get an honest read on what it would take to run it in your environment.

Not a fit if you are looking for a six-month exploration before naming a problem.

Teams that want a direct conversation, not a scripted sales process

If you want someone to do their homework, come prepared, and give you a straight answer instead of a rehearsed pitch, that is what I do. The best work I have done started with a single 30-minute call where both sides said what they actually thought.
