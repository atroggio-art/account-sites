About

The person behind the title.

I did not set out to sell software. I set out to solve real problems for businesses that make real things.

I am a Digital Transformation Architect at Salesforce, focused on manufacturers building the next generation of how they sell, market, and operate. Before this, I spent years in digital media sales, sitting across from business owners and learning something that most enterprise AEs never do: how to listen first and pitch second.

My background is in journalism, from the Cronkite School at Arizona State. It trained me to ask sharper questions than most, write with precision, and treat a polished presentation with exactly the skepticism it deserves. Those instincts matter more in this role than any product certification I have earned.

I live in Phoenix, Arizona. I am a dad first. That shapes how I work: with urgency and without noise. When we talk, I am not going to walk you through a deck designed for a different company. I am going to come prepared on your business and give you a straight read on what the fastest path to a real result actually looks like.

How I Work

An approach built around honesty over polish.

The best conversations I have are with leaders who are tired of sales theater. This is what I do instead.

01

I lead with your problem, not my product.

Every conversation starts with what you are dealing with. If there is a fit, it surfaces naturally. If there is not, I will tell you that too, and point you somewhere that might actually help.

02

I show you manufacturers who look exactly like you.

Not generic case studies. Companies in your segment, your size range, your go-to-market. What they deployed, how the first 90 days went, and what changed for the people doing the daily work.

03

I give you the real version of what deployment looks like.

Every project has complexity. I would rather surface it before you sign than have you discover it three months into an implementation that is already behind schedule.

04

I reach out when I have something worth your time.

Not to fill a cadence. When I contact you, there is a reason: a result from a company in your space, a new capability that connects to something we discussed, or a development worth your attention.

Who I Work With

The right fit matters. Here is what it looks like.

Not every company is the right match. I would rather tell you that upfront than spend both of our time on a conversation that goes nowhere.

01

Mid-market and enterprise manufacturers ready to move

Companies with 200 to 5,000 employees, a real revenue motion, and the organizational complexity that has outgrown spreadsheets and disconnected point solutions. You have the scale to get meaningful return and the urgency to actually do something about it.

02

Leaders who own the problem and have the authority to fix it

VPs and Directors of Sales, Marketing, Operations, and Finance who already know their current process is slower or less consistent than it should be. You can build the internal case. You just need the right outside perspective to start.

03

Companies that have moved past "we should look at AI" to "what does this actually look like for us"

You are not evaluating whether AI matters. You already know it does. What you need is someone who can show you a specific motion, in your industry, that has already proven itself, and tell you honestly what it would take to run it in your environment.

04

Teams that want a direct conversation, not a formal vendor process

If you want someone to do their homework, come prepared, and give you a straight answer instead of a scripted pitch, that is what I do. The best work I have done started with a single honest 30-minute call.
