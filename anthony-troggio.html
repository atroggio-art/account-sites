About

The person behind the title.

I did not set out to sell software. I set out to solve problems. The two ended up being the same thing.

I am a Digital Account Executive at Salesforce, focused exclusively on manufacturers across the Western US. Before that, I spent years in digital media sales, learning how to listen to business owners, understand what they actually need, and tell them what I honestly think. That habit did not change when I moved to enterprise software.

My background is in journalism, from Arizona State University. It taught me to ask better questions than most, write clearly, and never mistake a polished presentation for the truth. Those skills matter more in enterprise sales than most people expect.

I live in Phoenix, Arizona. I am a dad first. Everything else is second. That priority shapes how I work: directly, without wasting time, and with genuine care for the outcome on the other side of the conversation.

When I am working with a manufacturer, I am not trying to land a deal. I am trying to understand their business well enough to be genuinely useful to them, whether or not that leads anywhere in the short term.

What I Do

Where I spend my time.

I work with manufacturers who are trying to figure out how AI, automation, and connected data actually translate into results in their specific business. These are the three places where that conversation usually starts.

01

Revenue and Sales Operations

Helping sales teams quote faster, price consistently, and close deals without losing margin to manual processes. Complex configure-price-quote, automated contract workflows, and AI agents that handle the work between deals so reps can focus on the deals themselves.

02

Field Service and Operations

Helping operations teams get the right technician to the right job with the right parts, predict equipment failure before it happens, and build a service organization that runs on data instead of institutional memory and tribal knowledge.

03

AI and Agentforce

Helping manufacturers understand what AI agents actually do in the real world, where they create leverage, and how to start with a focused deployment that proves the model before expanding it. Not a pilot. A production system with a clear return.

How I Work

An approach built around honesty.

The manufacturers I work with best are the ones who value a straight answer over a polished one. This is how I try to earn that kind of conversation.

01

I lead with your problem, not my product.

Every conversation starts with what you are dealing with, not what I am selling. If there is a fit, it surfaces naturally. If there is not, I will tell you that too.

02

I show you manufacturers who look like you.

Not generic case studies. Companies in your space, your size, your go-to-market. What they deployed, how long it took, and what changed for the people doing the work.

03

I give you the honest version of what deployment looks like.

Every project has complexity. I would rather tell you that upfront than have you find it out three months into an implementation. The conversation is more useful when it is accurate.

04

I stay in contact because I think it matters, not to fill a cadence.

I reach out when I have something worth your attention. A result from a company in your space. A capability that just got released. Something that connects to a conversation we already had.

Who I Work With

The businesses where I can add the most value.

Not every company is the right fit, and I would rather tell you that than waste your time. Here is where I tend to create the most value.

01

Mid-market and enterprise manufacturers in the Western US

Companies with 200 to 5,000 employees, a real sales team, and operational complexity that has outgrown spreadsheets and disconnected systems. You have the scale to get meaningful return from automation and the urgency to move.

02

VPs and Directors of Sales, Operations, and Finance

The leaders who own quote-to-cash, field service, or revenue operations and already know there is a better way to run it. You have the authority to build the case internally and the context to evaluate whether what I am showing you is real or just a demo.

03

Companies evaluating AI seriously, not just watching it

Manufacturers who have moved past "we should look at AI" and are asking "what does this actually look like in our environment?" That is the conversation I am best equipped to have, and it is where I can point you toward the fastest path to a real result.

04

Teams that value a direct conversation over a formal sales process

If you want someone to listen, do their homework, and give you a straight read rather than a scripted presentation, that is what I do. The best work I have done has come from relationships that started with a single honest conversation.
