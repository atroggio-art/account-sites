Commerce Cloud · Top 10 Fits

These 10 companies sell through channels, catalogs, or dealer networks. That's Commerce territory.

B2B Commerce wins when buyers want self-service ordering, customer-specific pricing, and product catalogs they can search without calling a rep. Every account below checks at least three of those boxes.

#1 Composites One LLC Tier 1

The largest independent distributor of composite materials in North America, serving marine, wind energy, aerospace, and construction fabricators. Their customers — boat builders, wind blade shops, defense contractors — order the same resins, fabrics, and core materials on repeat cycles. Today that ordering is rep-driven and phone-based. B2B Commerce flips it to self-service with account-specific pricing and regional inventory visibility.

B2B Distributor Large Product Catalog Repeat Ordering Multiple Customer Segments Account-Specific Pricing
Composites Distribution "Your fabricators are ordering by email. Let them do it online."
#2 Standard Plumbing Supply Tier 1

A regional plumbing products distributor serving licensed contractors and plumbing shops. Contractors need to check stock, place orders, and track delivery against job timelines — all currently friction-filled. B2B Commerce gives each contractor their own punchout portal with saved job lists, contract pricing, and branch-level availability. The ROI story is direct: fewer will-call trips, fewer phone orders, faster jobsite fulfillment.

Contractor Portal Branch Inventory Trade Customer Tiers High Reorder Frequency
Plumbing Distribution "Your contractors call to check stock. That's a portal problem."
#3 FNA Group Tier 1

FNA manufactures Simpson and Delco branded pressure washers sold through big box retail, independent dealers, and direct to contractors. The real opportunity is parts and accessories — pump kits, hose reels, spray guns, detergent injectors — which are high-frequency, low-touch repeat purchases. A commerce site with registered dealer logins, bundled kits, and seasonal promotions captures revenue that currently goes to Amazon or the competitor's web store.

B2B2C Model Parts & Accessories Dealer Ordering Portal Branded Products
Power Equipment Mfg "Dealer parts orders are going to Amazon. Not anymore."
#4 Grimco Inc. Tier 2

National distributor of sign-making supplies — substrates, inks, vinyl, mounting hardware, display equipment — to sign shops, print houses, and graphics businesses. Their customers are small shops that order frequently across a massive catalog (10,000+ SKUs). Digital ordering is table stakes in this market; sign shops that can't order online at 11pm before a Monday install job go to a competitor who can. Commerce with customer-specific pricing and quick-order templates is a straightforward retention play.

10,000+ SKU Catalog Small Business Buyers 24/7 Order Urgency Competitive Market
Sign Industry Distribution "Sign shops order at 11pm. Your portal should be open."
#5 Mid-Am Building Supply Tier 2

Building materials distributor to homebuilders, remodelers, and contractors across the Midwest. Their buyers — framing crews, roofing contractors, general contractors — have project-specific material lists that repeat across jobs. B2B Commerce enables project-based saved lists, tiered contractor pricing, and delivery scheduling by job site. The key differentiator vs. competitors like ProBuild is the digital account experience that makes reordering frictionless for loyal contractors.

Designs and manufactures gearmotors, AC/DC motors, and speed controls sold through a national distributor network to OEM machine builders. Their products are highly configurable — gear ratio, voltage, frame size, shaft orientation — and today's selection/ordering process means spec sheets, phone calls, and long lead times. Commerce with a product configurator and distributor portal compresses that to a self-service spec-to-order workflow. OEMs win; Bodine's reps can focus on new applications.

Configurable Products Distributor Portal OEM Design-In Cycle Technical Buyers
Electric Motor Mfg "OEM specs a motor at 2am. Your configurator should handle it."

Manufactures and distributes poly bags, shrink film, stretch wrap, and custom printed packaging to industrial, food service, and retail customers. Packaging is one of the highest-frequency repeat purchases in any facility — operations managers reorder the same SKUs monthly. B2B Commerce with standing orders, volume tiers, and custom spec configuration (size, print, gauge) eliminates the rep touch on commodity reorders and frees sales to focus on upsell and new specs.

Their MRO parts business is the Commerce play: fleet operators need to order replacement parts, filters, and fluids against specific lift model numbers, and today that requires calling a service advisor or waiting for a counter visit. Manufactures commercial kitchen equipment — combitherm ovens, holding cabinets, smokers, blast chillers — sold through food service dealers to restaurants, hospitals, hotels, and universities. Four distinct audiences (corporate chefs, dealer sales reps, food service operators, service techs) each need a completely different message. Product launches require dealer enablement campaigns, chef demo programs, and operator ROI narratives running simultaneously. MCA handles this with audience segmentation, multi-step journeys, and behavioral triggers that no batch-and-blast system can replicate.

MCA maps it — triggered nurture for sample follow-up, re-engagement for gone-quiet prospects, event-driven campaigns around industry shows like IFT and Flavorcon, and segment-specific content for each buyer role in the chain.

6-18 Month Sales Cycle Multiple Buyer Roles Sample-to-Close Journey Event-Driven Campaigns
Flavor & Fragrance Ingredients "R&D requested a sample 4 months ago. Did anyone follow up?"
