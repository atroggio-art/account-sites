Our Point of View

In Pipe, the Product Wins
When the Specification Wins.

Diamond Plastics makes PVC pipe that engineers trust: NSF 61 certified, AWWA compliant, tested for the pressures municipal water systems demand. The product isn't the problem. The gap is in the 18 months before a project bids, when the engineer is deciding whose product name goes on the spec sheet.

"A municipal engineer doesn't switch pipe manufacturers because they found something cheaper. They switch because a competitor's rep delivered a technical guide 6 months before the spec was written, and Diamond Plastics wasn't there. The specification is the sale."

Diamond Plastics competes across three completely distinct audiences, and each one operates on a different timeline with different content needs. Municipal engineers are designing projects 12-18 months out and need technical validation: pressure ratings, soil compatibility, installation data, AWWA compliance documentation. Utility procurement teams are 6 months from bid and need delivery reliability and pricing structure. Civil contractors are 30 days from breaking ground and need installation guides, tech support contacts, and submittal documentation.

Today, staying present with all three depends on field reps managing individual relationships across a wide geography. Automated, segment-specific engagement changes the math: engineers stay informed through their entire design cycle, procurement teams get the right collateral at bid time, and contractors get hands-on support before the first section goes in the ground.

📐
If you're not in the spec, you're not in the bid

Municipal water and wastewater projects specify products by manufacturer name, standard, and performance requirement. That specification is written by an engineer who trusts the manufacturers that have educated them. Think technical guides, code compliance updates, installation webinars, and product bulletins. Automated content nurture for engineers is how Diamond Plastics earns specification by default, not by exception.

🏗️
Three audiences, three content tracks. One platform.

The engineer evaluating structural integrity, the procurement manager comparing delivery lead times, and the contractor running a trench are looking for completely different information at completely different moments. Segmented automation delivers the right content to the right audience at the right stage, without requiring three separate marketing teams to manage it manually.

💧
$55B in IIJA water infrastructure money is funding projects right now

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act directed over $55 billion to water and wastewater improvements. That money is being spent, which means engineers are designing projects, writing specifications, and selecting materials right now. The manufacturers who are in front of those engineers with educational content, compliance documentation, and product support are the ones getting specified into that spending wave.

📊
Marketing should tell sales which engineers are active, not the other way around

When an engineer downloads a pressure-class selection guide or attends a virtual lunch-and-learn, that's a buying signal. And it's happening 12 months before the RFP. Behavioral engagement data turns those signals into a rep-ready priority list: this engineer is designing a 16" transmission main, they've downloaded four technical documents, and they haven't been called in 90 days. Marketing hands sales the conversation, not just the contact.

Engineer Engagement: Win the Spec Before the Bid
  • Automated content journeys deliver technical guides, pressure-class selection tools, AWWA compliance documentation, and installation resources to municipal engineers throughout their 12–18 month design cycle
  • Behavioral scoring tracks which engineers are actively researching (document downloads, webinar attendance, website visits) and surfaces those signals to field reps before anyone else makes a call
  • Segment personalization distinguishes civil engineers from structural engineers from utility directors. Each gets content matched to their decision role, not a generic product blast
  • Regulatory and code update campaigns keep Diamond Plastics in engineers' inboxes when it matters most: when AWWA standards update, when NSF requirements shift, when new installation guidelines drop
Procurement Nurture: Be Ready When the RFP Lands
  • Bid-timing triggers activate when a project moves from design to procurement phase, automatically delivering pricing structure documents, lead time guarantees, and submittal packages before the procurement manager has to ask for them
  • Distributor engagement programs keep Diamond Plastics' channel partners armed with current product collateral, promotional pricing, and competitive positioning without relying on rep-to-rep phone calls
  • Post-bid follow-up sequences nurture awarded projects through delivery and installation, building the relationship for the next project before this one closes out
Contractor Support: The Relationship That Earns Repeat Specification
  • Pre-project onboarding sequences deliver installation guides, technical support contacts, and material handling instructions to contractors 30 days before groundbreaking, reducing field support calls and installation errors that damage the product's reputation
  • Project completion follow-up captures feedback, documents the installation, and sets the stage for the next project conversation. Contractors who feel supported specify the same products on their next bid
  • Campaign performance dashboards show which content, which audiences, and which project types are driving the highest specification rates, so you can continuously optimize the message and the timing
