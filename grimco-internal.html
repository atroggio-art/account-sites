Account Context
What makes Grimco different from a typical distributor pitch
Who they are

North America's leading sign supply distributor. Vinyl, wide-format media, LED, digital signage hardware, and substrates. They serve sign shops, print shops, and fabricators across the US and Canada from a network of distribution centers.

$105M+ revenue, 837 employees, HQ in Fenton, MO. They also acquired Graphic Solutions Group (Dallas, $100M) — more scale than the primary record shows.

The key insight

55% of orders already go online. That's exceptional for a specialty distributor. They built that. The pitch is NOT "go digital" — they're already there. The pitch is "connect what you built."

Their own 2020 opp notes said: "55% of our orders go online — there is a lot of data that we could leverage but we have historically been very old school." That's still the gap. Nothing has changed. They know it.

The data stack problem

HubSpot in the US. Constant Contact in Canada. 100k+ contacts. No unified customer record. $60k/month in ad spend with no loop back to order data.

Mohammad Masoud has "E-commerce" and "Marketing" in his title. That fragmented stack is his daily operational reality. He knows it's broken.

The loyalty program angle

In 2020 they were "about ready to launch a rewards program." It's 2026. It still hasn't launched at scale. The reason is the data infrastructure, not the marketing strategy.

A unified platform is the prerequisite. If you can connect the platform decision to the rewards program they've been trying to launch for six years, that's a compelling internal urgency driver they can take to the CEO.

Talk Track
Calibrated for Stage 03 — you're past discovery, you're validating value
The Stage 03 Framing

"You've seen the platform. The question I want to answer today is: what does success look like for Grimco specifically? Not what the platform can do in general — what are the two or three things that, if they were working differently six months from now, would make this investment clearly worth it?"

Get them to name their own success metrics. Then connect those metrics to the platform capabilities.

Discovery Questions (if needed)

What's the biggest thing holding the rewards program back right now?

When a sign shop who orders online goes 60 days without an order, what happens today?

How do you currently connect ad spend to the orders it generates?

What does a unified customer record across the US and Canada look like today, if it exists at all?

The ROI Frame

"Grimco already has 100k customer contacts and 55% of orders online. That's not a startup investment — that's infrastructure you've already built. What we're talking about is connecting it so it actually compounds."

"The math is simple: if a triggered replenishment email converts 5% of your lapsed accounts back to active, at average order values in your category, that number covers the platform cost in the first year. Want me to build that model with your actual data?"

The Close

"Here's what I want to propose: a scoped pilot that starts with your US email base and your online order data — just those two connected. No full migration, no Canada yet, no big infrastructure project. We get that live, you see the triggered campaign results, and you have real Grimco data to bring to the internal conversation."

Send the external briefing as a leave-behind after the call. It's built around their specific numbers and situation.

Objection Handling
The responses that keep a Stage 03 deal moving forward
We're happy with HubSpot. It does what we need.
HubSpot is a great marketing tool. The gap isn't what it does for email — it's what it can't see. HubSpot doesn't know what your customers ordered last week, which sign shops are trending down in purchase frequency, or which ad click turned into a $5k order. When your marketing platform can see your order data, it stops being a campaign tool and starts being a retention engine. That's the conversation.
Migrating 100k contacts is a huge project. We don't have the bandwidth.
You don't start with 100k contacts. You start with the US base, connected to your order data, with three automated journeys: replenishment for lapsed accounts, cross-sell at checkout, and a welcome series for new sign shops. That's a 90-day scoped deployment. Canada comes later, and it comes after you've seen the results from the US. The migration is a phase, not a prerequisite.
We already looked at Salesforce Marketing Cloud before and it didn't go anywhere.
I know — two conversations went to No Decision since 2020. What I want to understand is what "no decision" actually meant. Was it budget, was it integration complexity, was it that the internal case didn't land? Because the platform has changed significantly since 2020, and more importantly, what Grimco has built since then — the 55% online, the customer data at scale, the data science function — means the ROI case is materially different today. I'm not asking you to re-evaluate what you evaluated before. I'm asking you to evaluate what's possible now.
Our sign shop customers don't engage with automated email.
That's true for generic batch-and-blast email. It's not true for a replenishment trigger that tells a sign shop their usual vinyl is back in stock at their nearest DC, or a message that shows them the laminate that pairs with the substrate they ordered two weeks ago. The open rates on behavior-triggered email in B2B distribution run 3-4x higher than batch campaigns. The difference is the data connection — which is exactly what this conversation is about.
We need to get internal alignment before we can move forward.
That's fair. Let me help you build the case. If I put together a one-page ROI model using Grimco's actual numbers — 100k contacts, current order frequency, average order value — you'll have something concrete to bring to the conversation. The rewards program angle is particularly compelling internally because it's a visible customer-facing outcome, not just a backend infrastructure project. Who else needs to be in the room when you have that conversation?
