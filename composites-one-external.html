Our Point of View

Your Competitive Advantage Is Your Breadth.
Your Gap Is How Customers Access It.

Composites One carries more product SKUs, serves more end markets, and has more distribution centers than any competitor in North America. The challenge is that most of your fabricators still order by phone or email. Every order your inside sales team takes manually is one they could have spent on a relationship, a cross-sell, or a new account.

"A fabricator who trusts Composites One will still place a reorder through whoever is easiest to buy from at 11pm. Loyalty doesn't override convenience. A portal closes that gap."

Composites One's depth of product and regional distribution is genuinely hard to replicate. But fabricators in marine, wind, and aerospace don't order during business hours. They order when the work demands it. If they can't check your inventory and place a PO at midnight, they'll call whoever answers first in the morning.

A B2B self-serve portal with account-specific pricing, real-time inventory across your DCs, and PO-based checkout captures that demand under your brand. Your inside sales team stops being an order desk and starts spending time on the accounts and conversations that actually move the needle.

🏭
Fabricators in your key verticals don't order 9 to 5

Marine fabricators are on the water at dawn. Wind energy crews are in the field. Aerospace shops run night shifts. These are not customers who wait for your counter to open. A portal that gives them real-time stock visibility across your 60+ DCs and lets them check out with a PO at any hour captures the demand you're currently losing to whoever picks up the phone first.

💰
Account-specific pricing online is how you keep margin direct

Your largest fabricator accounts have negotiated pricing, volume tiers, and stocking agreements. Right now, a rep has to be in the loop on every reorder to make sure the right price is applied. A portal that shows each account their exact pricing on login eliminates pricing disputes, speeds up the order cycle, and keeps your customers ordering direct instead of going to a spot market competitor.

📊
Manufacturing Cloud tracks what your reps are managing in spreadsheets

Your biggest accounts have volume commitments, annual stocking agreements, and project-based demand that your commercial team is tracking manually today. Manufacturing Cloud gives you account-level run rate visibility, demand deviation alerts, and a connected forecast that shows your ops team what's coming before it hits the DC. That's the difference between reactive replenishment and planned distribution.

🔗
Connected commerce and operations is the play your competitors can't copy fast

Any distributor can stand up a website. What takes time to build is the connection between a customer's portal experience and your actual inventory, pricing, and account agreements. When those two things are connected on Salesforce, a fabricator ordering online gets the same accuracy as a rep-assisted order. That operational trust is what earns the reorder and the next project, not just the first transaction.
