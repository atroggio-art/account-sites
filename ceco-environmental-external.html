Our Point of View

Two Industrial Companies. One Commercial Platform.
The Integration Window Closes Faster Than You Think.

The synergies in an industrial acquisition don't show up on their own. They get built into the systems decisions made in the first 90 days. CECO has 45 of those days behind it. The question is whether the next 45 set up one unified commercial operation or lock in two siloed ones for the next three years.

"Acquisitions don't fail because the products don't fit. They stall because two separate quoting processes, two CRM systems, and two pricing engines make it impossible to see the combined pipeline clearly. The revenue synergies are right there. The system to capture them has to be built while the integration window is open."

CECO's sales pipeline exceeds $6.5 billion across air pollution control, fluid handling, and now Thermon's industrial heating and process safety products. Those are complementary product lines with overlapping end-markets. A refinery that buys CECO's emissions control equipment is exactly the kind of customer who needs Thermon's heat tracing on the same plant. The cross-sell opportunity is obvious. But cross-selling across two systems, two CRMs, and two quoting processes doesn't happen. It gets talked about on earnings calls and buried in integration spreadsheets.

The integration timeline is the constraint. The decisions being made right now about systems, pricing, and pipeline visibility will define what "unified" actually means for CECO's commercial team in 2027. Companies that standardize on one platform during the integration window see the synergies in year one. Companies that let two systems coexist find themselves doing this conversation again in two years, with a harder migration and more organizational scar tissue.

⚙️
Unified quoting across CECO and Thermon product lines is where the margin lives

CECO's engineered-to-order equipment and Thermon's process safety and heating products have different quoting complexity. When a rep can quote both product lines from one system, with one approval workflow and one pricing engine, the quote-to-close cycle shortens and margin leakage from manual pricing exceptions drops. That is a direct line from system decision to synergy capture. Two quoting systems mean two sets of exceptions, two approval chains, and two data sets your pricing team can't reconcile.

🏆
$6.5B in pipeline means nothing if you can't see it clearly from one dashboard

Your CRO is accountable for $1.3B in combined revenue. If pipeline visibility requires toggling between two CRM systems and reconciling two forecast methodologies, the number your CRO presents to the board is already out of date. A unified pipeline view isn't a nice-to-have in a post-acquisition year. It's the instrument panel you need to see whether the integration is actually tracking toward the revenue guidance you've already given the market.

📊
The cross-sell opportunity between CECO and Thermon is real and time-limited

Refineries, petrochemical plants, and industrial facilities that are already CECO customers are also Thermon prospects. The window to introduce Thermon products to CECO's existing book of business is widest in the first year post-close, when the acquisition is still a talking point and customers expect to hear about the combined portfolio. That window closes as accounts go quiet and the combined sales organization settles into its old patterns. A unified customer view is what turns "we have a combined portfolio" into actual cross-sell pipeline.

📋
The systems decisions made in the first 90 days are the ones that stick

Integration momentum is highest in the first quarter post-close. Sales organizations are in flux, processes haven't hardened, and there's genuine organizational appetite to simplify. Six months in, that window closes. People have adapted their workarounds, territories have been set, and changing systems becomes a political problem on top of a technical one. The conversation CECO's leadership needs to have about commercial platform standardization is not a 2027 conversation. It is happening right now.

The companies that wait see them, if at all, in year three.
