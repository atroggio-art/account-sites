Our Point of View

You Built the Digital Foundation.
Now Connect It.

Grimco did the hard part: you convinced sign shops to order online. Most distributors are still trying to do that. Your gap now is that your online ordering, your customer data, and your marketing engine are running in separate systems. When those three things connect, everything changes.

"55% online is a remarkable achievement for a specialty distributor. But the next 35% doesn't come from better UX. It comes from knowing what your customers are about to need before they know it themselves."

A sign shop that ordered vinyl wrap last month is about to run out. A print shop that bought a new wide-format printer three months ago needs ink and media. A fabricator who hasn't ordered in 60 days has probably found another source. These are all signals sitting in your order data right now that nobody is acting on.

Connect that order history to a marketing platform that can send the right message to the right shop at the right time. Connect it to a B2B commerce experience that surfaces the right products on login. That's how you get from 55% to 90. Not by asking customers to change their behavior. By meeting them exactly where they already are.

📦
Order data is your most valuable marketing asset. It's sitting unused.

Every sign shop that orders from Grimco leaves a behavioral fingerprint. What they buy, how often, in what quantities, with what seasonality. Right now that data lives in your commerce system and never touches your marketing platform. When it does, you can trigger replenishment emails before a shop runs out, surface complementary products at checkout, and identify at-risk accounts before they churn.

🗄️
Two email platforms means two customer universes. That's a structural problem.

HubSpot in the US, Constant Contact in Canada. 100k contacts split across two systems with no unified profile. When a sign shop customer in Toronto moves their business to Seattle, your teams have no way to connect that. A rewards program or loyalty initiative has no hope of working across a fragmented database. One platform, one customer record, one view of the relationship across every touchpoint.

📣
$60k a month in ads is a lot to spend without knowing who converted

You're running Google, Facebook, and LinkedIn ads to drive sign shops to grimco.com. But when one of those shops clicks an ad and places an order, does your ad platform know? Does your email platform? Right now those are disconnected events. Connect ad spend to order history and you'll know exactly which campaigns are driving revenue and which are burning budget. That's the conversation your marketing team wants to have but doesn't have the data to drive.

🏆
The rewards program is the unlock. One platform makes it possible.

Grimco has been working toward a rewards or loyalty program for years. The reason it hasn't launched at scale isn't marketing budget or creative. It's data infrastructure. A loyalty program that runs off a unified customer record, connected to real-time order history across every DC and channel, is an entirely different product than one built on top of a fragmented CRM. The platform decision you make now determines whether the rewards program is a real retention tool or a one-off campaign.
