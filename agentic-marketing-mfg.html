The Real Problem
Why Most Manufacturing Marketing Runs Blind
You have better data than any B2C company on the planet. Orders, contracts, product mix, service history. You just cannot get to it from your marketing tool. That is the entire problem.
Your most valuable signals are locked in your ERP
Every order, every contract date, every product line a customer has ever bought. It is in SAP or Dynamics. Your marketing tool has none of it. So campaigns go to the wrong people at the wrong time, based on who opened an email last month. Not on who is actually buying and what they are buying next.
You are marketing to accounts, not contacts
B2B manufacturing deals involve 5 to 15 stakeholders per account. A procurement manager is not the same as a plant manager or VP of Operations. Most marketing tools treat them as one contact on a list. That mismatch kills relevance and tanks engagement before a single email lands.
Long sales cycles need nurture that actually keeps running
Manufacturing deals take 6 to 18 months. No team can manually manage a nurture sequence over that timeline. Most leads either get pounded in week one or forgotten by month three. Automated journeys tied to real account behavior close that gap without adding headcount.

Tactical Playbook
5 Plays Manufacturing Marketers Are Running Right Now
These are not concepts. These are specific motions live at manufacturing companies today, mapped to the capabilities that run them.
Play 01
Lapsed Buyer Reactivation, Running Automatically
A customer who has not ordered in 90 days triggers an automated journey: a personalized email referencing their last order category, a follow-up SMS from their rep, and a task for inside sales if there is still no response at day 14. Zero manual effort. Runs 24 hours a day. Most manufacturing teams lose this revenue silently because nobody has time to pull the list every week.
Agentic Marketing + Unified Buyer Data
Play 02
Contract Renewal Runway: 90/60/30 Day Sequence
Unified buyer data ingests contract dates from your ERP. When a contract hits 90 days out, a renewal journey launches automatically. Value summary at 90 days. Case study at 60. Direct outreach from the AE at 30. Marketing and sales moving as one coordinated motion, not a last-minute scramble driven by whoever remembered to check the spreadsheet.
Agentic Marketing + Unified Buyer Data + CRM
Play 03
New Product Launch to a Relevant Audience Only
Instead of blasting a product announcement to your full list, unified buyer data segments by purchase history, industry, and product affinity. Customers who buy related products get the launch email. Everyone else does not. Engagement goes up. Unsubscribes go down. The product actually launches to the buyers most likely to care about it.
Agentic Marketing + Unified Buyer Data
Play 04
Inbound Inquiries Answered Overnight, Without a Person
A prospect fills out a quote request at 9pm. An AI agent responds immediately. It answers product questions, qualifies the opportunity, and either books a meeting or routes to inside sales with a full summary. No lead sits cold overnight. GE Appliances is resolving 25% of all customer interactions this way with no human involved at all.
AI Agents + Agentic Marketing
Play 05
Account-Based Marketing for Your Top 50 Accounts
Your top 50 accounts get a fundamentally different experience than the rest of your list. Unified buyer data scores engagement and opportunity signals. Automated journeys trigger personalized content based on what that account is actually doing: visiting pricing pages, opening service emails, ordering new SKUs. Marketing becomes a revenue signal, not just brand noise.
