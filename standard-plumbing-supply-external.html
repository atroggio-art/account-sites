Our Point of View

Your Relationships Win on the Job Site.
They Shouldn't Have to Win on the Phone Too.

Standard Plumbing Supply's advantage isn't the catalog. It's the relationships, the local expertise, and the ability to pull a hard-to-find fitting when a job is on the line. That's not something Ferguson's website can replicate. But contractors are still choosing Ferguson online for routine orders because it's easier. The fix isn't better salespeople. It's a better ordering experience.

"A contractor who loves doing business with you will still place a 6am reorder through Ferguson if that's the easier path. Loyalty doesn't override convenience. A portal does."

The contractors who buy from Standard Plumbing Supply aren't switching because Ferguson has better prices or better product. They're splitting their wallet because Ferguson makes it easy to order without calling anyone. Account-specific pricing online, PO-based checkout, real-time inventory, and order history they can reference on a job site.

A direct B2B portal gives your contractors all of that under your brand. The relationship stays yours. The margin stays yours. And every self-serve order is one less call your counter staff has to take.

🔧
Contractors order outside business hours. You need to be open.

Job sites start early and run late. The contractors who plan their next day's materials are doing it the night before or first thing in the morning. If your portal isn't open, their order goes somewhere that is. A 24/7 self-serve channel captures demand at the moment it happens, not when your counter opens.

💰
Account pricing online is the one thing that keeps orders direct

Contractors don't want to call to get their tier pricing. They want to log in, see their number, and check out. A portal that shows each contractor their negotiated rate eliminates the main reason they shop around. It also reduces pricing disputes and the time your team spends quoting repeat orders.

📦
Real-time inventory is a competitive weapon

One of the biggest reasons contractors call your counter is to check if something is in stock. Put that information online. A contractor who can see that you have 40 units of a fitting on the shelf right now doesn't need to call Ferguson to check. That visibility is a reason to stay.

📋
Job-based ordering changes how contractors buy

Contractors don't just want to reorder SKUs. They want to order by job, see what they've bought for each project, and reorder a full material list with one click. A portal built around the way contractors actually work turns routine orders into locked-in purchasing behavior that's very hard for a competitor to displace.
