Who They Are

Standard Plumbing Supply Company, Inc. is a $364M, 644-person wholesale plumbing distributor headquartered in Sandy, UT. They serve contractors, builders, and showroom customers across the West through both a contractor supply business and branded showrooms.

Family-owned by the Reese family: Richard Reese is President, Jacob Reese is VP, Benjamin Reese is Director. This is a multi-generational business where platform decisions require family buy-in, not just a budget approval.

The Changed Signal

Devon Chandler now holds the title Director of E-Commerce at a $364M plumbing distributor. That role did not exist when the prior Salesforce conversation happened. It is a direct signal that Standard Plumbing is actively working on a digital channel strategy.

He is not a stakeholder to introduce to digital commerce. He is already working on it. The question is whether Salesforce becomes the platform behind what he is building, or whether he builds it on something else.

The Competitive Threat

Ferguson is the primary competitor Standard Plumbing calls out on their own external site. Ferguson has a fully digital ordering channel at national scale: account pricing, real-time inventory, 24/7 self-service.

Standard Plumbing's competitive edge is relationships and service depth. But that edge erodes when a contractor can get similar products with a better ordering experience at 6am before the counter opens. The relationship advantage is real but it is not permanent without a digital layer.

Why Now

Devon Chandler's title is new. There is a window to engage before he commits to a platform. Once a decision is made elsewhere, it is a multi-year lock-in.

Loyd Steffensen (VP of Marketing and IT, most recently active Jul 2, 2026) owns both the marketing stack and technology decisions. He is active right now. The prior opp was a small $18,750 "GetOnTheBoard" test, not a real evaluation. This conversation starts fresh with the right people and the right scope.

Discovery Questions

Value Pivot

"Ferguson has a 24/7 ordering platform with account pricing. Your contractors know that. The ones who stay with you do it because of the relationship and service. But if placing a standard order is easier with Ferguson, the relationship advantage starts to erode."

"A contractor portal does not replace your counter team. It captures the demand that was going to Ferguson at 6am. The counter team handles the complex orders and the relationship work they were hired to do."

Show the Westlake Royal dealer portal as the structural analog. It demonstrates contractor account pricing, real-time inventory, and job-based ordering in a building products distributor context.

Send the external briefing as a leave-behind after the call. Ask for a second meeting that includes Loyd Steffensen or Jacob Reese if Devon Chandler wants to move forward.

Objection Handling
The five objections most likely to come up with this account
Our contractors prefer the counter experience.
For complex orders and technical questions, yes. For a routine reorder of a SKU they have ordered 20 times? They prefer whatever is fastest. The portal handles the routine. The counter handles the relationship. Counter staff generally like it because they stop being order entry clerks and start being service advisors. You are not replacing the counter. You are freeing it up.
We are already building something in-house or with another vendor.
Tell me more about where that is headed. The question I would ask is whether contractor account pricing is connected to the online experience, or whether it is a catalog with generic pricing. That is the piece most regional distributors build wrong the first time, and it is the difference between a portal your contractors actually use and one they do not. If you are mid-build, it is worth a 20-minute look at how we solved that problem for a comparable distributor before you lock in an architecture.
Ferguson is too big to compete with on digital.
You are not trying to out-Ferguson Ferguson on price or product breadth. You are trying to give your contractors, the ones who chose you for service and relationships, a digital experience that is as good as Ferguson's for the transactions that do not need a relationship. The contractors who are loyal to Standard Plumbing will use your portal if it exists. Right now they are using Ferguson's because it is the only one available at 6am.
We do not have budget for a platform project right now.
The ROI math for a contractor portal is straightforward. If your top 100 contractor accounts shift 20% of their reorders to self-serve, the counter labor savings and order capture from off-hours demand pays for the platform in year one. I can build that model using your actual order volumes before we talk about budget. Give me a number to work with and I will come back with a model you can take to leadership.
The prior Salesforce conversation did not go anywhere.
The prior conversation was a small starter at $18,750. It was a relationship test, not a real platform evaluation. This is a different ask: a contractor ordering portal scoped to your top accounts with account pricing and real-time inventory. Different scope, different stakeholders, different business case.
