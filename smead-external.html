Our Point of View

Smead Has the Brand.
The Right Platform Makes It Work Harder.

Smead wins on product reliability, catalog depth, and retail reach. The gap that every established B2B brand faces is that brand recognition doesn't automatically translate to digital loyalty — especially when procurement leads are making decisions on Amazon and Office Depot before your team ever gets a call.

"A procurement manager who reorders the same Smead file folders every quarter is your most valuable customer. Right now, they're probably doing it on Amazon — and you're not capturing the relationship, the data, or the margin."

Marketing Cloud Advanced changes that equation. Every reseller interaction, direct purchase signal, and lapsed buyer becomes a trigger for a personalized, automated engagement — without your marketing team building lists by hand. And when a corporate account places a volume order through Office Depot, B2B Commerce gives them a reason to come direct next time: account pricing, custom catalogs, and order history in one portal they actually want to use.

🏢
Retail channels generate data that disappears

Every Office Depot and Amazon order is a signal — product preference, reorder cadence, volume tier. Without a connected platform, that intent data never makes it back to marketing or sales. Data Cloud captures it and makes it actionable.

🔁
Reorder cadence is predictable — but not automated

Office supplies have the most reliable reorder patterns in B2B commerce. A procurement manager who hasn't ordered in 45 days is a risk. Automated re-engagement journeys trigger on purchase history before the relationship goes to a competitor.

💼
Corporate accounts deserve a real B2B portal

Procurement teams at mid-market and enterprise companies need account-specific pricing, custom product catalogs, PO-based ordering, and order history — not a consumer checkout. A purpose-built direct ordering portal turns that capability into a competitive advantage over Amazon.

📣
New product launches deserve more than a mass email

When Smead launches a new organizational system, the office managers most likely to adopt it are already in your database. Behavioral segmentation by purchase history, company size, and buying role means the right buyers get the launch — not everyone.
