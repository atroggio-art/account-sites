Our Point of View

The Hardest Part of M&A Isn't the Deal.
It's Making Three Companies Operate Like One.

KPS, Everidge, and SRC all make cold storage equipment, but they have different customer lists, different sales motions, and different data. Until those are unified, leadership is flying blind and the field team is working harder than it needs to.

"The fastest path to realizing value from the Everidge and SRC acquisitions is getting one unified view of customers, pipeline, and field operations. Before the integration window closes."

Post-acquisition, the window to build the right foundation is narrow. Salesforce gives KPS a single operating platform across all three entities, so sales teams can see cross-sell opportunities, service teams can dispatch intelligently, and leadership has one dashboard instead of three spreadsheets.

Every month the three customer lists stay separate is a month of duplicate tooling spend, missed cross-sell between KPS, Everidge, and SRC accounts, and field reps working from partial information. The highest-intent window for capturing that value is the first 12 months post-close. Not after.

🏢
Three orgs, one customer record

When KPS, Everidge, and SRC customers overlap, your teams shouldn't be tripping over each other. A unified CRM gives every rep full visibility into the customer relationship, not just their slice of it.

This isn't a rip-and-replace of what Everidge and SRC already run. It's a unified layer built on top. Legacy systems wind down on your timeline, not ours.

Your field team is scaling. Don't let dispatch become the bottleneck.

KPS is bringing on a large wave of field technicians. Without intelligent scheduling and mobile job access, the growth you're investing in will be eaten up by coordination overhead. Field Service + Agentforce solves this before it becomes a problem.
