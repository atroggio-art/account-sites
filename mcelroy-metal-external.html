Our Point of View

Your Dealers Know Your Product.
Your Contractors Should Be Able to Find It Themselves.

Metal roofing is a specified purchase. Before a contractor calls anyone, they research profiles, compare options, and build a material list. If McElroy is hard to find and configure at that stage, a competitor's product that is easy to find gets specced instead.

"A metal roofing contractor bidding a commercial job doesn't grab whatever panel is available. They choose a profile. They spec a gauge. They check lead times. All of that happens before they pick up a phone."

The dealers who win specs are the ones whose products are easiest to evaluate digitally. A contractor building a bid at 9pm is not going to call a dealer at that hour. They are going to find what they can find online, build their preliminary material list, and move forward. If that search surfaces a competitor's configurator and not McElroy's catalog, the spec is already decided before anyone opens their office in the morning.

McElroy's products are best-in-class. The discovery and spec experience needs to match. A digital configurator that lets a contractor pick a profile, choose a color and gauge, enter a length, get pricing, and check lead times in under three minutes is not a convenience feature. It is how you protect your spec position in every market your dealers serve.
